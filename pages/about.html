About

The story behind the strategy.

I help teams turn complex ideas into clear strategies and stories that drive real-world impact.

Location San Francisco Bay Area
Current Role Product Marketing, Google Maps Platform
Education MBA, Columbia Business School
Undergrad BA Political Science, Yale University

I'm a bridge-builder at heart.

I'm a product marketing leader who loves helping teams figure out what really matters — and how to tell that story clearly. I've spent the last several years at Google partnering across teams to turn complex technical visions into products people actually want to use.

I thrive in the "in-between" spaces — bringing clarity to moments when a product is powerful, but the story isn't obvious yet. My superpower is creating alignment across different teams so everyone moves in the same direction.

With an MBA from Columbia Business School and a Political Science degree from Yale University, I've built my career on moving with speed and precision while always staying grounded in the user's perspective. My path through Meta, Coursera, and Google has given me a unique lens — I understand what it takes to market products at every stage, from scrappy startup to global scale.

Outside of work, I care deeply about building opportunity for people and businesses. I serve as the Chair of the Economic Development Advisory Commission for my city, where I focus on community growth and long-term economic vitality. I believe the same strategic thinking that drives tech growth can — and should — be applied to making communities stronger.

On a personal note

I'm an early-morning reformer Pilates enthusiast, a firm believer that starting the day in your body (instead of your head) changes everything. I love celebrating Lunar New Year traditions, building routines over resolutions, and connecting with people who are building something meaningful.
